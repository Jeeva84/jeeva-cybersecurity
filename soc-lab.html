SOC Home Lab

A complete SOC lab I built from scratch — configuring networks, simulating attacks, forwarding logs, monitoring with Splunk, scanning with Nessus, and investigating with Autopsy.

Download the video (MP4)

Setup

  • VMware Workstation with custom VMnets for segmented VLANs.
  • pfSense firewall as gateway with manual inbound/outbound rules (fixed Kali internet issue).
  • Windows 10 (endpoint), Ubuntu (server/log forwarder), Domain Controller.
  • Splunk for log monitoring, Nessus for vulnerability scanning, Autopsy for forensics.

Commands Used

sudo /opt/splunk/bin/splunk add monitor /var/log/auth.log -sourcetype linux_secure -index main -auth admin:Johnathan@21
hydra -l jeeva -P /root/top10.txt ssh://10.0.10.25

Conclusion

This project represents more than just a technical exercise — it reflects my passion and dream to work in cybersecurity. Every troubleshooting step, every log parsed, and every attack simulated taught me persistence and real-world problem-solving. Building this SOC lab is a foundation I’m proud of and a step closer to my goal of becoming a skilled cybersecurity analyst.
